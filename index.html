⭐ Recommended Vaccine Schedule (Birth–6 Years) Routine childhood vaccines and what they help prevent
Birth
Newborn
  • Vitamin K – 1 dose
    Prevents dangerous bleeding in newborns, including bleeding in the brain which can result in brain damage or, in severe cases, death.
  • HepB – 1st dose
    Prevents hepatitis B infection, which spreads through contact with infected blood or body fluids and can result in chronic liver disease, cirrhosis, liver cancer, or, in severe cases, death.
  • RSV (Nirsevimab) – if born during or entering RSV season and maternal RSV vaccine was not given during pregnancy
    Prevents severe RSV lung infection, which can result in breathing failure or, in severe cases, death in infants.
1 Month
Routine visit
  • HepB – 2nd dose
    Prevents hepatitis B infection, which can lead to lifelong liver disease or, in severe cases, death.
2 Months
Can start as early as 6 weeks
  • DTaP – 1st dose
    Prevents diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis (whooping cough). These infections can block breathing, damage the heart, cause severe muscle spasms, pneumonia, brain injury, or, in severe cases, death.
  • IPV (Polio) – 1st dose
    Prevents polio, which can cause permanent paralysis or, in severe cases, death.
  • Hib – 1st dose
    Prevents serious bacterial infections including meningitis, which can result in brain damage or, in severe cases, death.
  • PCV (Pneumococcal) – 1st dose
    Prevents pneumonia, bloodstream infection, and meningitis, which can result in severe illness or death.
  • Rotavirus – 1st dose
    Prevents severe diarrhea and vomiting, which can cause dehydration requiring hospitalization.
  • RSV (Nirsevimab) – if under 8 months entering RSV season and not yet given
    Prevents severe RSV lung infection, which can result in respiratory failure or, in severe cases, death.
4 Months
Routine visit
  • DTaP – 2nd dose
  • IPV – 2nd dose
  • Hib – 2nd dose
  • PCV – 2nd dose
  • Rotavirus – 2nd dose
6 Months
Routine visit
  • DTaP – 3rd dose
  • IPV – 3rd dose
  • Hib – 3rd dose (if required by vaccine type)
  • PCV – 3rd dose
  • HepB – 3rd dose
    Prevents hepatitis B infection, which can result in chronic liver disease or death.
  • Influenza – yearly starting at 6 months
    Prevents flu infection, which can result in pneumonia or, in severe cases, death.
  • COVID-19 – yearly
    Prevents severe COVID-19 illness, which can result in respiratory failure or, in severe cases, death.
  • RSV (Nirsevimab) – if under 8 months entering RSV season and not yet given
    Prevents severe RSV lung infection, which can result in respiratory failure or, in severe cases, death.
9–11 Months
Seasonal vaccines
  • Influenza – yearly
    Prevents severe flu infection, which can result in pneumonia or death.
  • COVID-19 – yearly
    Prevents severe COVID-19 illness, which can result in respiratory failure or death.
12–15 Months
Toddler vaccines
  • MMR – 1st dose
    Prevents measles, mumps, and rubella. These infections can cause pneumonia, brain swelling, meningitis, hearing loss, and serious birth defects during a future pregnancy.
  • Varicella (Chickenpox) – 1st dose
    Prevents chickenpox, which can cause pneumonia, brain inflammation, severe skin infection, or, in severe cases, death.
  • HepA – 1st dose
    Prevents hepatitis A, which spreads through contaminated food, water, or hands and causes liver inflammation.
  • Hib – final dose
    Prevents serious bacterial infections including meningitis, which can result in brain damage or death.
  • PCV – 4th (final) dose
    Prevents pneumococcal infections including meningitis and bloodstream infection, which can result in severe illness or death.
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
15–18 Months
Routine visit
  • DTaP – 4th dose
  • HepA – 2nd dose
    Prevents hepatitis A infection affecting the liver.
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
Year 2 (24 Months)
Annual vaccines
  • Influenza – yearly
    Prevents severe flu infection, which can result in pneumonia or death.
  • COVID-19 – yearly
    Prevents severe COVID-19 illness, which can result in respiratory failure or death.
Year 3 (36 Months)
Annual vaccines
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
Year 4 (48 Months)
Booster vaccines
  • DTaP – 5th dose
  • IPV – 4th dose
    Prevents polio-related paralysis.
  • MMR – 2nd dose
  • Varicella – 2nd dose
    Prevents chickenpox infection, including severe complications.
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
Year 5 (60 Months)
Annual vaccines
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
Year 6 (72 Months)
Annual vaccines
  • Influenza – yearly
  • COVID-19 – yearly
